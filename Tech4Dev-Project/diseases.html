Disease Information

Communicable and Non-Communicable Diseases

Communicable diseases: These diseases are caused by infectious agents and can be transmitted from person to person or through vectors.

Non-communicable diseases: These diseases are not caused by infectious agents and cannot be transmitted from person to person.

Communicable Disease Information

Communicable diseases are illnesses that spread from one person to another or from an animal to a person, or from a surface or a food. Communicable disease include Neglected, Tropical and Vector Borne Diseases. Tuberculosis. Coronavirus infections. Dengue. Hepatitis. Climate Change and Health. Measles. HIV/AIDS.

Non-Communicable Disease Information

The term NCDs refers to a group of conditions that are not mainly caused by an acute infection, result in long-term health consequences and often create a need for long-term treatment and care. These conditions include cancers, cardiovascular disease, diabetes and chronic lung illnesses.
